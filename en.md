## Approach
- Centered on **data sovereignty for individuals** and **explainability**, with attention to the full pipeline (design → prototype → evaluation) toward real-world implementation.
- Treating documents and knowledge **as graphs** to balance comprehension and reusability.
- Tackling challenges of an aging society through data × UX, emphasizing **incremental empirical validation**.

### [Joint Research with HEROIC Inc.](https://heroic.co.jp/pdf/tamagawa_press.pdf)
**Research on Engagement Evaluation Using Multimodal Sensing Information for Visualizing Human Capital**

Developing methods to quantitatively evaluate the engagement of "human capital"—the source of corporate competitiveness and sustainable growth—using multimodal sensing information including biometric, behavioral, and environmental data. The project aims to build a new framework that applies evaluation results to corporate human-resource strategy and investment decisions.

## Research Themes

### 1) Personal Data & Personal AI
Personal data management based on distributed PDS, and exploration of Personal AI (PAI) dedicated to individuals. Includes operational design, consent management, and evaluation methods in educational and regional contexts.

**Keywords:** PDS / PAI, Data Governance, Consent, Privacy-by-Design

### 2) Graph-Document for Learning & Work
**Visualization and reuse of thinking** through collaborative graph-document authoring. Structuring for learning, training, and internal knowledge, along with quality design for RAG I/O.

---

### 3) Multimodal/Observational Sensing
**State understanding and evaluation** through multi-perspective observation including audio, video, and behavior. Oriented toward **lightweight measurement** that fits naturally into on-site operations.

**Keywords:** Multimodal, Engagement, Aging Society, Human Factors

## Crowdfunding

### <a href="https://camp-fire.jp/projects/703754/view" target="_blank">Building a Case Database of Musculoskeletal Pain Patients in Acupuncture Clinics Using Electronic Medical Records</a> (Domestic Joint Research, Implemented via Crowdfunding)
- **Purpose**: Collect cases of musculoskeletal pain patients across multiple acupuncture clinics via electronic medical records, building a research foundation that accumulates and disseminates clinical evidence.
- **Funding / Support Achievements**:
  - Raised **¥1,395,289** from **240 supporters**, far exceeding the ¥300,000 goal (465% achieved)
- **Achievements at Implementation**:
  - Established a system enabling pilot case collection at approximately 10 facilities; electronic medical record system ready for operation
  - Conducted interim reports and academic presentations at venues including the Japan Society of Acupuncture and Moxibustion, advancing while incorporating on-site feedback
- **AI Implementation**:
  - Designed case data in a form usable by AI for natural language processing, symptom classification, and outcome prediction models
  - Building a foundation for collecting high-quality data through input-efficiency improvements and data standardization for electronic medical records
